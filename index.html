Water Pollution

What is Water Pollution?

Water pollution is the release of toxic substances into bodies of
water. This make water unsafe for human use (drinking, etc) and disrupt aquatic
ecosystems (coral ecosystems, etc). Water pollution can be caused by many
different contaminantsincluding toxic waste, plastic, microplastics and
disease-causing micro-organisms.

How To Help Solve Water Pollution?

To reduce water pollution we can properly dispose of oils and other chemicals, use
eco-friendly cleaning materials, minimize your plastic use, don't flush
non-flushable items like medicines, conserve water and use natural drain cleaners.

What Are We Doing?

We are making a prototype for a 'Smart Dam'. The Smart Dam is basically a dam that
collects water throughtout the day. Then every night it drains out the water
leaving only the trash. Then the trash is emptied out onto a platform where it gets
segregated based on its type (Example: Plastic waste is seperated from fabric).
Then all the plastic is compressed into bricks used for construction of bus stands
and benches. The rest of the waste is donated to NGOs for their use (Fabric is used
clothes and metal for bowls etc). We believe this might actually help Water Pollution decrease.
